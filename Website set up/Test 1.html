About

Welcome! I am a full-time Research Assistant (RA) in the Economic Policy Studies Department at the American Enterprise Institute (AEI) for Public Policy Research. I primarily support the research of PhD economists and Senior Fellows Stan Veuger, Vincent Smith, and Paul Kupiec. My research focuses on the intersection of labor, urban, and public finance, with additional interests in the integration of causal inference and machine learning methods. I earned my BA in the Quantitative Economics Concentration from the Department of Economics at the University of Hawaii at Manoa in summer 2024. Before joining AEI, I worked as an RA at the University of Hawaii Economic Research Organization (UHERO), a Research Fellow at the Center for Entrepreneurship and Economic Education at Hawaii Pacific University, and a Research Associate with the Grassroot Institute of Hawaii. I plan to pursue a PhD in economics and/or public policy in the near future.
